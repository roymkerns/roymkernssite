The Author

Roy M. Kerns

Hymns of the Hadrian Quadrant

Roy M. Kerns is the author of The Azar Project, the first installment in an ongoing sci-fi trilogy that spans millennia, star systems, and the fragile threads that hold humanity together against the dark.

Set thousands of years in the future, Roy's debut novel follows Dr. Mayakannan — a scientist forced to choose between compliance and catastrophe at the edge of known space — and the haunted crew she has to hold together to do it.

Roy is a trans man from the Philly area who somehow found himself on the other side of the world in Nagoya, Japan.

His mother craved hoagies during her pregnancy with him, so it was kind of prophetic that he worked at a Wawa making hoagies for five years before skedaddling off to Japan to teach English. Working in countless customer-facing jobs and auditing for the Census somehow better prepared him to manage a classroom than school did.

He loves cats, karaoke, TRPGs, spicy food, mangoes, and making shitty art of his characters. He is also a hopeless extrovert who genuinely, truly loves people, even when they drive him crazy.

And he sincerely hopes that his writing conveys that.

Currently writing — The Karava Initiative, Book Two of the Xerxes Trilogy.
